## Background

Computation is happening all around us. Modern society depends heavily on
generally available computers of different kinds, from smartphones to laptops to
web servers and mainframes.

This course provides a foundational, general understanding of computation along
with practical skills, including

- theory (different _models_ of computation and their fundamental limits),
- design (conceiving your own _hardware_ and _software_),
- application (building software _applications_ by programming),
- computer literacy (how to purchase, build, and use a typical _personal computer_), and
- software development (bringing software applications to market).

!!! note
The term "computer science" is often used to describe theory, design, and
application, or a subset of those three.
<%= courseCode %> is not a typical computer science course---it covers more
ground.
!!!

### Course description

<%= courseCode %> is a 12-week, online course.
Each week, a one-hour lecture is offered over Zoom. Every two weeks, a
problem set comes due. Your solutions to the problem sets will be graded by
the instructor and given a grade of A, B, B-, C, C-, or D.
At the conclusion of the course, if an average grade of C- or higher is
achieved, you will receive a certificate of completion. Students who achieve
a grade of A or B are eligible for an instructor's letter of recommendation.
